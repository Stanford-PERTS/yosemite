Dear Teachers,

Thank you for your school’s work in the fall to help implement PATHS+. For the final portion of the program this year, your 9th grade students will again complete an online survey about academic motivation, self-esteem and social anxiety. The step-by-step instructions you will need to introduce and facilitate this program are included in this document. Please take a few minutes to review these instructions and have them with you when your class(es) are scheduled to participate. We have also included some helpful tips regarding troubleshooting and FAQs, so you know what to do if any issues or questions come up while your students are participating.

Because high classroom and student completion rates are important to the goals of this study, students who do not participate on the day the sessions are planned will be asked to attend a make-up session. Please use the attached “Student Make-Up List” to indicate which students did not participate and the reason why. Instructions are included on that form.

Please follow the instructions below. The survey will take approximately 30-40 minutes to complete.

Instructions

Step 1

All participating students should have an Internet-connected device to complete the survey, and only one student should be using each device. Any students who do not have parental permission to participate should be provided with an alternate activity while the rest of the class goes through the survey.

Step 2

Read the following instructions to the class.

“Our school is participating in a survey developed by the University of Texas at Austin. Today, our class will complete an online survey that may help teachers and administrators at our own school and researchers better understand how to make school a better place for all students. The survey questions are designed to help us understand more about the way you, as high school students, think and learn and how you feel in regards to academic motivation, self-esteem and social anxiety.

“Participating in this survey is voluntary and your grade in this class will not be affected whether you complete the survey or not. You may skip any question or survey you do not wish to answer or complete. When responding, choose the answer that best fits what you know or feel. Don’t pick a response just because you think that’s what someone wants you to say. Only a limited number of students are participating in this survey, and the answers you give are very important so the results are useful.

“I would like to emphasize that this is not a test of you or this school. Remember: we will use what we learn to improve students’ experience here and at other schools as well. So please read the instructions carefully and do your best to answer all of the questions to the best of your ability. If participating in today’s activity raises any questions or concerns for you, please contact Mr./Ms. _____________ at the school.

“Finally, it is very important that you not talk to each other or look at each other’s screens. That would be a violation of privacy, and I cannot allow it. No one at our school will look at your specific answers.

“We’ll get started in just a moment. When you finish, please remain quiet so others can finish too.”

Pause here to answer any questions.

Step 3

Write the website address and the code phrase on the board where all your students can see it. Distribute the student sign-in cards that were prepared for you by your School Coordinator. If any student does not have a sign-in card (e.g., new student), they can still participate. Instruct them to go to the URL, enter the code phrase, and then select this class from the drop down menu. They should then follow the instructions on the screen.

Read the following:

“Now open your browser and please go to studentspaths.org/go. Enter the phrase ‘[[ cohort.code ]] 1’ and follow the on-screen instructions.”

“Please be aware that as you move through the survey, you may not go back to a previous question. The browser’s back button won’t work during the survey. This protects your privacy by never displaying your previous answers to anyone.

“Use the drop down menu and select the class shown on the sign in card I just gave you. Then, enter your full first and last names in the appropriate boxes as shown on your card. If you have a nickname you prefer to use, you can enter it, but you must also enter your first and last name.

“Please begin.”

Step 4

As the students are completing the survey, please complete the Student Make-Up List to indicate which students on your roster are not participating and the reason why not. Return this form to your School Coordinator at the end of the survey, or no later than the end of the school day.

Write on the board the name and contact information of the school counselor or another school mental health professional to whom students with questions can be referred.

Step 5

At the end of the class period, ask your students to log out by closing their browser window.

“We have reached the end of the survey. It’s okay if you are not totally done with the survey, just close the browser.

“Thank you. The information you have provided may be used to improve students’ experience here and at other schools as well. If participating in today’s activity raises any questions or concerns for you, please contact Mr./Ms. _____________ listed on the board.

Troubleshooting

We work hard to minimize technical problems, but sometimes issues come up. Here are some problems students have experienced in the past and the best ways to solve them.

For all school-related issues please contact your schoolâ€™s Study Coordinator.

Closed Page

Problem: A student closed the browser window.
Solution: Tell the student to go to studentspaths.org/go and sign in again, just like they did the first time. The survey will continue where the student left off.

Warning Message or Connection Expired Message

Problem: A student’s screen says that the connection expired or a similar warning.
Solution: Click the “reload” or “refresh” button.

Stuck on a page

Problem: A student can’t get past a certain page in the survey.
Solution: Hold down the control key (labeled "Ctrl" or "control") and press the y key. This will advance the survey to the next page.

Lost Location

Problem: The survey restarted from the beginning.
Solution: Tell the student to skip up to the part where they left off (don't worry; the answers the student previously provided have been saved).

Student receives the message "You have already taken this survey" or similar

Problem: The student is given a message with a reason why they cannot access they survey.
Solution: There are many reasons this might happen. Most of the time the message is correct, e.g. the student already took this or a later survey. However, if you and the student determine that the message is incorrect and they should still participate, the student can sign in again under a different name. For example, “Mindy Patel” could instead sign in as “Mindy Patel-Different”. When the computer asks if the student is really “Mindy Patel” select “None of the above” and then the student will be able to access the survey. The new name can later be linked with the student’s correct account.

Cannot Connect to studentspaths.org/go

Problem: Students cannot access the sign-in page when they go to studentspaths.org/go.
Solution: Check if they can access another website, like google.com.

  • If not, there is a problem with your Internet connection. Talk to technical support staff at your school to resolve the issue.

A Student Completed the Study in Another Class

Problem: A student tells you they have already completed the survey in another class.
Solution: The student should NOT do the same session of the study more than once. Ask the student to work on something else quietly or you may let them leave quietly.

Some Students Ran Out of Time

Problem: Some students do not complete the survey.
Solution: That is fine. Just ask them to get as much done as possible. They may be asked to try to complete the session on a later date.

Answers to Student Questions

  • Do I have to do this?
    Your opinions are important, so I hope that you would at least try.
    If a student refuses again, just accept this. You should add them to the Student Make-Up List as “student refusal,” as they may change their mind and be interested in participating at a later date.
  • Why should I do this?
    Because all of my students are doing this today, and we will learn important information.
  • Will I get a grade on this?
    It’s not graded. But, when you provide honest answers, it will help faculty to do a better job teaching students like you in the future.
  • Why do I have different questions from him/her?
    Different students are doing different activities. The survey has a lot of different activities, but there’s not enough time for everyone to do all of them. You should do the ones you were assigned.
  • Does spelling and grammar matter?
    No, just concentrate on getting your ideas across.
