Instructions for [[ cohort.name ]]

Sessions 1 and 2


Dear Teachers,

During the fall semester, your 9th grade students are being given the opportunity to participate in a program that uses the latest evidence-based methods to positively affect student attitudes about learning. The instructions you will need to introduce and facilitate this program are attached. Please take a few minutes to review these instructions and bring them with you when your class(es) are scheduled to participate. We have also included some helpful tips regarding troubleshooting and FAQs, so you know what to do if any issues or questions come up while your students are participating.

Because high classroom and student completion rates are important to the goals of this study, students who do not participate on the day the sessions are planned will be asked to attend a make-up session. Please use the attached “Student Make-Up List” to indicate which students did not participate and the reason why. Instructions are included on that form.

We hope this program is helpful for your students, however, please do not discuss this expectation with your students. That is, we would appreciate it if you would not emphasize that it might be helpful for them. Instead, please emphasize how much the researchers need their input and that their opinions are valuable.

In addition, unless you suspect that a student is experiencing difficulty and needs support, please do not ask students anything about their experience—whether they liked it, what they thought about it, what they wrote about, etc. Also discourage students from sharing information about it with their peers. You can say, “Let other students experience it for themselves.”

Thank you for your participation!
The Students' PATHS Researchers

Session Instructions

Sessions 1 will take about 45 minutes; Session 2 will also take about 45 minutes. Please follow the instructions below for each session.

Step 1

All participating students should have an Internet-connected computer to complete the activity, and only one student should be using each computer. Any students who do not have parental permission to participate should be provided with an alternate activity while the rest of the class goes through the program.

Step 2

Read the following instructions to the class.

“Our school is participating in a program developed by Stanford University. Today, our class will participate in the [first or second] of two online activities that may help teachers and administrators at our own school and researchers at Stanford better understand how to make school a better place for all students. The activities are designed to help us understand more about the way high school freshman think and learn. You’ll be answering survey questions, reading some short passages, and answering some questions about what you read.

“Participating in this activity is voluntary and your grade in this class will not be affected whether your complete the activity or not. You may skip any question or activity you do not wish to answer or complete. When responding, choose the answer that best fits what you know or feel. Don’t pick a response just because you think that’s what someone wants you to say. Only a limited number of students are participating in this program, and the answers you give are very important so the results are useful.

“I would like to emphasize that this is not a test of you or this school. Remember: we will use what we learn to improve students'experience here and at other schools as well. So please read the instructions carefully and do your best to answer all of the questions to the best of your ability.

“Finally, it is very important that you not talk to each other or look at each other's screens. That would be a violation of privacy, and I cannot allow it. No one at our school will look at your specific answers.

“We’ll get started in just a moment. When you finish, please remain quiet so others can finish too.”

Distribute headphones.

“Some sections of the program have audio, so you will need to use headphones. Each of you is receiving headphones to listen to these segments without disturbing other students. These headphones are yours to keep and use for both sessions.”

Students can also use their own personal headphones, according to school policy, if it’s not disruptive to the class to retrieve them.

Pause here to answer any questions.

Step 3

Write the website address and the code phrase where all your students can see it. Distribute the student sign-in cards for the appropriate session that were prepared for you by your School Coordinator. If any student does not have a sign-in card (e.g., new student), they can still participate. Instruct them to go to the URL, enter the code phrase, and then select this class from the drop down menu. They should then follow the instructions on the screen.

If this the first session with the students, read the following:

“Now open your browser and please go to studentspaths.org/go. Enter the phrase ‘[[ cohort.code ]] 1’ and follow the on-screen instructions.”

If this the second session with the students, read the following:

“Now open your browser and please go to studentspaths.org/go. Enter the phrase ‘[[ cohort.code ]] 2’ and follow the on-screen instructions.”

For both sessions, read the following:

“Please be aware that as you move through the program, you may want to go back to a previous question. If so, do not use the back arrow on the browser. Instead, use the “Go Back” link in the upper left corner, just above the questions.

“Use the drop down menu and select the class shown on the sign in card I just gave you. Then, enter your full first and last names in the appropriate boxes as shown on your card. If you have a nickname you prefer to use, you can enter it, but you must also enter your first and last name.

“Please begin.”

Step 4

As the students are completing the program, please complete the Student Make-Up List to indicate which students on your roster are not participating and the reason why not. Return this form to your school coordinator at the end of the session, or no later than the end of the school day.

Step 5

Ten minutes before students need to finish, tell them how much time is remaining and that it is okay if they do not have time to finish everything.

“We have about ten minutes remaining in the class period. Please continue with the activity, but do not rush. It is okay if you do not have time to finish everything. You can log back in to the program at a later date.”

Step 6

At the end of the class period, ask your students to log out by closing their browser window.

“We have reached the end of the session. It's okay if you are not totally done with the activity, just close the browser.

“Thank you. The information you have provided may be used to improve students' experience here and at other schools as well.

“If you have any questions related to your participation in this study, please contact Alice Roberts, Project Director, at ICF International, toll-free at 1-844-225-6089.”

Troubleshooting

We work hard to minimize technical problems, but sometimes issues come up. Here are some problems students have experienced in the past and the best ways to solve them.

If there is a time-sensitive problem that you need assistance with, please call 1-844-225-6089. For all school-related issues please contact your school’s Study Coordinator.

Closed Page

Problem: A student closed the browser window.
Solution: Tell the student to go to studentspaths.org/go and sign in again, just like they did the first time. The program will continue where the student left off.

Warning Message or Connection Expired Message

Problem: A student's screen says that the connection expired or a similar warning.
Solution: Click the “reload” or “refresh” button.

Stuck on a page

Problem: A student can't get past a certain page in the survey.
Solution: Hold down the control key (labeled "Ctrl" or "control") and press the y key. This will advance the survey to the next page.

Lost Location

Problem: The survey restarted from the beginning.
Solution: Tell the student to skip up to the part where they left off (don't worry; the answers the student previously provided have been saved).

Student receives the message "You have already taken this survey" or similar

Problem: The student is given a message with a reason why they cannot access they survey.
Solution: There are many reasons this might happen. Most of the time the message is correct, e.g. the student already took this or a later survey. However, if you and the student determine that the message is incorrect and they should still participate, the student can sign in again under a different name. For example, "Mindy Patel" could instead sign in as "Mindy Patel-Different". When the computer asks if the student is really "Mindy Patel" select "None of the above" and then the student will be able to access the survey. The new name can later be linked with the student's correct account.

Cannot Connect to studentspaths.org/go

Problem: Students cannot access the sign-in page when they go to studentspaths.org/go.
Solution: Check if they can access another website, like google.com.

  • If not, there is a problem with your Internet connection. Talk to technical support staff at your school to resolve the issue.
  • If you can connect to google.com but you cannot connect to studentspaths.org/go, wait for a few minutes and try again. If the connection still does not work, call 1-844-225-6089 to speak with a study researcher. If you do not reach someone immediately, please leave a message and someone will call you back promptly.

A Student Completed the Study in Another Class

Problem: A student tells you they have already completed the survey in another class.
Solution: The student should NOT do the same session of the study more than once. Ask the student to work on something else quietly or you may let them leave quietly.

Some Students Ran Out of Time

Problem: Some students do not complete the survey.
Solution: That is fine. Just ask them to get as much done as possible. They may be asked to try to complete the session on a later date.

Audio Issue

Problem: Student cannot hear the sound.
Solution: Look for a speaker cone symbol in the upper right or lower right corners of the screen for volume controls. Check that the computer is not muted, that the volume is turned up high enough, and that the headphones are fully plugged in.

Answers to Student Questions

  • Do I have to do this?
    Your opinions are important, so I hope that you would at least try.
    If a student refuses again, just accept this. You should add them to the Student Make-Up List as “student refusal,” as they may change their mind and be interested in participating at a later date.
  • Why should I do this?
    Because all of my students are doing this today, and we will learn important information.
  • Will I get a grade on this?
    It’s not graded. But, when you provide honest answers, it will help faculty to do a better job teaching students like you in the future.
  • Why do I have different questions from him/her?
    Different students are doing different activities. The program has a lot of different activities, but there’s not enough time for everyone to do all of them. You should do the ones you were assigned.
  • Does spelling and grammar matter?
    No, just concentrate on getting your ideas across.
