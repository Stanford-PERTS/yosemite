Study Title: Internet-based classroom motivation and performance research

This is the official study information form for adults. If you are under 18, please skip to the bottom section of the form.

STANFORD UNIVERSITY Research Consent Form

Protocol Director: Dave Paunesku

DESCRIPTION:
You are invited to participate in a research study. This study explores how different educational activities and views about school affect motivation and academic outcomes. If you choose to participate, you will complete several online activities. In the course of completing these activities, you may practice academic skills like reading or mathematics or answer questions about yourself or others in school. We are interested in how different individuals may benefit from different kinds of activities.

We are also interested in determining if and how the study activities affect people's academic outcomes or those of individuals they interact with in the school setting. By participating in this research, you grant consent to your school to share any academic and/or disciplinary records with our research team for the duration of the study protocol. That information will be kept confidential; it will not be shared with anyone outside the research team in a way that could be used to identify you.

RISKS AND BENEFITS:
There are no risks associated with this project. We cannot and do not guarantee or promise that you will receive any benefits from this study. Your decision whether or not to participate in this study will not affect your relationship with your school. The knowledge gained from this study will help teachers, coaches, and parents help increase students' motivation.

TIME INVOLVEMENT:
Your participation in this study will consist of 1-10 sessions lasting 20-50 minutes each.

PAYMENTS:
You will not receive any payment for your participation.

DATA STORAGE AND PROTECTING CONFIDENTIALITY:
All confidential data we receive will be kept in electronic spreadsheets or databases and stored in encrypted folders on study staff password protected computers or in password protected databases. All identifiable information, like names, will be replaced with unique identification numbers before data are exported from secured environments for analysis. Identifiable data will not be shared with anyone outside the trained research team. All confidential data will be deleted when no longer needed for the purposes for which the study was conducted.

SUBJECT'S RIGHTS:
If you have read this form and you do not wish to participate in this project, you should inform your supervisor, the protocol director, or simply not complete the study activities. Please understand your participation is voluntary and that you have the right to withdraw your consent or discontinue participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. You have the right to refuse to answer particular questions. Your privacy will be maintained in all published data resulting from the study.

STANFORD UNIVERSITY Research Consent Form

Protocol Director: Dave Paunesku

  1. What will happen to me in this study? (Description of the study)
    During the study, we'll ask you to tell us what you think about school, learning, and yourself. You'll have the opportunity to participate in an online program that will involve writing about yourself, completing some questions, and/or learning how you can study more effectively. We will also ask your teachers some questions about you. This will include getting your grades and data about your behavior in class.
  2. Can anything bad happen to me? (Risks or Discomforts of Participating)
    Nope; everything you'll be doing will be completely safe.
  3. Can anything good happen to me? (Benefits of Participating)
    Hopefully, you'll find the program interesting. Also, you will help us learn more about what students think and how they learn. This information will be used to help make school a better place for all students to learn.
  4. Who will know I am in the study? (Confidentiality)
    Your parents, your teachers, the other students in the study, and the researchers at Stanford who are in charge of the study will know that you're in it. The researchers at Stanford will know how you respond to the questions we ask you. Your teachers will know how you respond to some of the questions, and we will always tell you which questions your teachers will be able to see.
  5. Who can I talk to about the study? (Contact Information)
    If you have any questions about the study or any problems to do with the study you can contact the Protocol Director, XXXXXXXXXXXXX. You can call him at XXXXXXXXXXXX. You can also email him at XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX.

    If you have questions about the study but want to talk to someone else who is not a part of the study, you can call the Stanford Institutional Review Board (IRB) at (650)-723-5244 or toll free at 1-866-680-2906.
  6. Do I have to participate? (Voluntary Participation)
    You don't have to participate. We hope that you will enjoy participating, but you can stop at any time. Just tell your teacher that you don't want to continue in the study, and you won't have to.
  7. How will you store data to protect confidentiality?
    All confidential data we receive will be digital and will be kept in electronic spreadsheets stored in encrypted folders on study staff password­-protected computers or in password­-protected databases. When we receive confidential data it is immediately stripped of identifiable information. For example, names are replaced with a unique identification number. Identifiable data will not be shared with anyone outside the trained research team. All confidential data will be deleted when no longer needed for the purposes for which the study was conducted.
  8. How will results be used?
    After we finish our project, we will write reports describing what we have learned from working with you and your classmates. All your information will remain strictly confidential. We will present the aggregate (whole school) data to your teacher and principal as well as to the broader research community.
